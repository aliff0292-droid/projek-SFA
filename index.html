The Agrotech Challenge in Malaysia

Optimizing Hormonal Forcing Windows for MD2 Pineapples

In Malaysia's highly lucrative pineapple sector (concentrated in Pontian and Kluang, Johor), MD2 cultivation requires precise scheduling. Forcing—the artificial induction of flowering using ethylene-releasing chemicals (Ethephon)—must be executed when the vegetative plant biomass reaches a critical mass of 2.5 kg to 3.0 kg.

If we force too early, fruits are small and unmarketable. If we force too late, growers waste fertilizer, labor, and crop cycles on redundant vegetative growth. To pinpoint this physiological sweet spot, agrotech managers model growth using the **Logistic Growth Function $f(t)$** and analyze its **first derivative $f'(t)$** to track biological acceleration, peak metabolism, and growth deceleration.

Mathematical Formulation

1. Plant Biomass Function $f(t)$

Models total vegetative plant weight (grams) at month $t$:

$$f(t) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(t - t_0)}}$$

2. Growth Rate / Derivative $f'(t)$

Using the quotient and chain rules, the instantaneous growth rate (g/month) is:

$$f'(t) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(t - t_0)}}{\left(1 + e^{-k(t - t_0)}\right)^2}$$

Growth Velocity Index ($k$): 0.45 / month
Influenced by fertilizer efficiency, soil health, and rainfall.
